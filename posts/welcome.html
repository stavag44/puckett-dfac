Standing Orders

Ok, assholes.

I've felt pretty honored the last couple of days since this channel was created and, without my knowledge or consent, named with a nod to me. Food is the only thing that keeps me sane in this world. It's my passion, my creative outlet, and my way to teach.

So I'm moving my shit to a blog (yeah, I know) and I'll link to it here and in the chow-hall channel whenever I update. I'm not trying to monetize or get traffic. I just want to keep track of what I'm doing.

What I really want is to get more of you motherfuckers into cooking at home, because it is literally life changing. I wifed a woman ten times out of my league because I can consistently put a great meal on the table.

Get in your kitchen. Go buy some weird shit. Read recipes. That's literally all I did. I kept at it for thirty years and ended up running a restaurant in San Francisco that I've since sold. Now I want to teach.

Don't be intimidated by what I post or what anyone else posts. Put up your most basic stuff and your worst mistakes. That's how you learn. Ask questions. Seek knowledge. That's why I'm here.

Chef Stavag

I'm a former U.S. Army reconnaissance officer, combat veteran, and trained chef. After leaving the military I opened a restaurant in the San Francisco Bay Area, where I learned quickly that running a kitchen you love and running a business you love are two completely different things. I closed the doors, went back to cooking for the right reasons, and never looked back.

I cook as a serious hobbyist now. No tickets, no covers, no food cost. Just the pursuit of getting better. This site exists to help you do the same. Whether you're just starting out or already comfortable in the kitchen, there's always a next level. That's what we're here for.

Colonel Ralph Puckett Jr.

Commanded the 8th Army Ranger Company during the Korean War. On November 25, 1950, he led 51 Rangers against a fortified hill held by several hundred Chinese soldiers — deliberately drawing enemy fire across open ground three times to protect his men.

Wounded multiple times during six consecutive counterattacks, he refused evacuation until his Rangers were clear. His Distinguished Service Cross was upgraded to the Medal of Honor in 2021, presented by President Biden. He died in April 2024 — the last surviving Korean War Medal of Honor recipient.

This site bears his name. 1st Ranger Battalion traces its lineage through his company. Rangers Lead the Way.
